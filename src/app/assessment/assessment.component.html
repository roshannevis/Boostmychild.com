How assessments work?

Why regular assessments are necessary?

  • Assessments help to know how the child has reached in terms of their development and experiences.
  • They help determine how to build in activities to encourage/extend the onward growth.
  • In the event of a delay early intervention helps as prompt action /support can be taken and the problem prevented from being multiplied. 

Development Areas

The areas of learning and development are all vital and are inter-connected. Especially in early years, it is important for a child to make the required progress in each of these to develop curiosity and enthusiasm towards learning and growth.

1. Physical

Physical development is about keeping children active and to develop their co-ordination, control, and movement. Children are made aware of various kinds of physical activity, about eating healthy and keeping themselves hydrated.

2. Communication, Language and Literacy

Children need to be provided opportunities to experience a rich language environment; develop their confidence and skills in expressing themselves; and to speak and listen in a range of situations. It also involves encouraging children to link sounds and letters and to begin to read and write. Children must be given access to a wide range of reading materials like books, poems, charts to ignite their interest.

3. Social and Emotional

Involves helping children to develop a positive sense of themselves, and others; to form positive relationships and develop respect for others; to develop social skills and learn how to manage their feelings; to understand appropriate behavior in groups; and to have confidence in their own abilities.

4. Problem Solving & Reasoning

It involves providing children with opportunities to develop and improve their skills in counting, understanding and using numbers, calculating simple addition and subtraction problems; and to describe shapes, spaces, and measures.

5. General Knowledge & Exposure

Involves guiding children to make sense of their physical world and their community through opportunities to explore, observe and find out about people, places, technology and the environment.

6. Arts & Creativity

Involves enabling children to explore and play with a wide range of creative material, as well as providing opportunities and encouragement for sharing their thoughts, ideas and feelings through a variety of activities in art, music, movement, dance, role-play and technology.

 Assessment Process:

Boostmychild experts have created assessments for each of the above development areas. Each one contains a set of questions, which ask you about activities that your child may be doing in the area. You just need to answer ‘yes’, ‘sometimes’, or ‘not yet’ to each question. Your answers are scored, and we provide you with a cumulative score, together with other useful feedback, at the end of each assessment. You can take each assessment as many times as you like with a 30 day gap between each assessment retake. This process accurately helps monitoring growth and progress for your child on a month to month basis.
